The Challenge

Your mission is clear.
The operations aren't.

You built something worth protecting. These are the pressures quietly working against it, and against the people you're trying to serve.

No Standardization

Every staff member doing it differently means quality lives or dies by whoever is on shift. Your clients deserve better odds than that.

Leadership & Clinical Disconnect

When leadership and clinical teams operate in silos, staff burn out, decisions stall, and the people in your care feel it first.

Clinicians Without Boundaries

Staff who don't feel backed to hold the clinical line will bend it. Clients get less than they need, and your team carries the weight of it.

Documentation & Compliance Gaps

Payer audits, licensing surveys, and accreditation visits don't wait for you to get organized. Gaps in documentation and unclear compliance protocols create real financial and legal exposure.

The Real Cost of Inaction

Every month without structure, your staff improvise. Every improvised decision is a place where a client can fall through the cracks. The best people you hired came to do meaningful work, not to reinvent the wheel every day.

The how should be decided once. So your team can focus entirely on the who.

How I Help

Three interventions.
One clear outcome.

We build the systems that steady your team, mark the path, and make sure no one gets left behind: client or clinician.

Operational Standardization

We document the how once: roles, protocols, and processes. Your team operates with consistency and confidence every single day. No more reinventing the wheel. No more quality that depends on who's on shift.

→ Clean documentation means defensible claims and fewer denials. Practices lose 10–20% of revenue to preventable billing errors. Standardized workflows close that gap.

Clinical Boundaries & Staff Empowerment

We build the frameworks and culture that give your clinicians the tools, language, and backing to hold the clinical line, even when it's hard. Staff who know their lane deliver better care and stay longer.

→ Accurate level-of-care placement protects revenue and reduces compliance risk. Misplacement means either underbilling or payer scrutiny. Both cost you. ASAM documentation is your first line of defense.

Leadership Alignment

We close the gap between leadership and clinical teams, building communication structures where decisions are clear, voices are heard, and everyone moves in the same direction toward the mission.

→ When clinical and ops teams align, census grows and staff stay. Replacing one clinician costs an average of $46,000. Retention is your highest-ROI investment.

About

Laura Poulter, LCSW

Laura has spent the majority of her career inside behavioral health and recovery. Through her clinical work at Harris House Foundation in St. Louis and The Meadows Outpatient Centers in Austin, and building her own private practice at UnMuck LLC, she has worked inside the systems, alongside the staff, and with the clients that make this work matter.

At The Meadows, she led clinical protocol design for a partial hospitalization program, developing standardized assessment SOPs that improved patient placement accuracy by 43% and reduced adverse outcomes by 20% across 300+ cases. At Harris House Foundation, she led enterprise-wide EHR implementation across five departments and 200+ clinical staff, translating complex provider workflows into systems that achieved a 30% improvement in data accuracy while maintaining full regulatory compliance. She is also Google Project Management certified, meaning the operational rigor she brings is backed by formal systems training, not just instinct.

She started Pathstone because she believes the organizations doing the hardest, most important work deserve operations that hold them up, not slow them down. Her goal is simple: to connect, improve, and support sustainable systems every day she sees the sun.
